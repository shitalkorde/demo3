Tata Group origins and history

Jamsetji Tata established a trade business in 1870 with a capital of 21,000 INR. He bought a failing oil building in Chinchpokli, converted it into a cotton plant apprehended as Alexandra Mill, and then sold it at a profit two years later. He established the Empress Mill, a new cotton plant in Nagpur, in 1874. His goals were to build a hydropower plant, a world-class learning organisation, a world-class iron and steel corporation, and an extraordinary resort. The Taj Mahal Hotel at Colaba waterfront was inaugurated in 1903 during his lifetime, making it the first in prominence for a large amount of time.
